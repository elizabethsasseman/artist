This project displays a flow of mockups leading a user to plan a leave of absence from work for a family health condition. Shanti Gupta - one of our personas - is our user in this flow and she is planning a leave of absence for her daughter, Saavni Gupta. Shanti is planning her leave through an absence management portal set up for her through work.

In presenting our designs, we use personas to tell a story in order to make the situation seem as real as possible to stakeholders and non-designers. Presenting in this way especially helps sell our designs to stakeholders. This storytelling process is in itself a user-friendly way to describe the reasoning behind our design decisions to non-designers, and to visually portray how we've incorporated requirements into our designs.

Requirements & Challenges

The business need was to redesign the Plan a Leave feature, and to include the Login and Logout pages in the redesign. A multitude of new features were to be introduced that our designs would account for. The following is a list of project requirements:

  • Include seasonal images on the login and logout pages
  • Remove additional text from the login page and simplify into one item
  • Ensure design of login and logout pages are consistent
  • Add helpful items to Plan a Leave splash page
  • Incorporate images into splash pages for all Plan a Leave types
  • Rework the Plan a Leave progress bar
  • Incorporate [this list] of additional questions into the Plan a Leave flow in locations that make sense
  • Design new radio buttons to align more closely with our brand
  • Ensure all user flows are accounted for, including [these specific items] that need to be reworked; for example, certain fields should not exist as an option for certain scenarios
  • Change return to work date to expected return to work date
  • Include the option to resume later
  • Create a page to review information before submitting
  • Include ability to edit information before submitting
  • Include information about what happens next after a leave request is submitted
  • Rework the landing page for easier comprehension and intuitive access to leave requests, leave details, leave tracker, and tasks.
  • Simplify leave details page to remove unnecessary items, and reorganize layout

The following is a list of problems throughout the design process that we ran into and how we overcame them:

  • Finding seasonal images, and spash page images that created the right feeling for people likely going through a difficult time was challenging. It was also difficult to find images that had the potential to relate to anyone logging in. We decided to find images that weren't too specific, that did not include faces, that were inclusive of all genders and races, and that evoked a sense of calm, peace, and positivity.
  • Incorporating the list of additional questions for all leave types into the flow was difficult. Finding out what questions only pertained to specific situations was challenging because some of these situations had never been seen before. There are many complex situations in the world of leave requests. We created MVP versions of our designs to account for the complex nature of this user flow, and did the best we could at finding answers to what might happen in some of the unique situations users find themselves in.
  • Reworking the progress bar and including the ability for a user to resume later and go back to edit a response created scope creep for the engineers. We had numerous discussions with them to determine whether this feature could be included in some MVP fashion or not. We reworked our designs in order to decrease the amount of scope creep that was happening because of this requirement.

Login Page

Shanti Gupta needs to plan a leave of absence from work to care for her sick daughter, Saavni. Shanti arrives at the login page of the portal and has a very pleasant initial impression. It's currently springtime and the vibrant image she sees of tiny plants gives Shanti a brief moment of joy in thinking about her own vibrant garden. Shanti can use all the joy she can get these days with everything going on. Shanti notices a huge difference in how welcoming the site is now compared to how it was when she last logged in. Shanti goes ahead and logs in.

Below is how this screen used to look.

Landing Page

Upon arriving on the main landing page, Shanti immediately notices a leave that is currently open for herself. The gray checkmarks remind her she still needs to complete one of her tasks, although she is not here to do this right now. Shanti scans the page and quickly notices a blue button that says, "Plan a Leave". She clicks it assuming without a doubt that this will take her to the location where she will be able to plan a leave for Saavni. The last time Shanti logged in here it took her what felt like forever to figure out how to plan a leave.

Splash Page

Shanti is taken to a page with a few friendly reminders. This helps her confirm that she has everything she needs in order to successfully plan her leave. Shanti determines she has all the correct items and clicks, "Begin".

Plan a Leave

The following mockups show Shanti's flow through each page in filling out information related to her leave for her daughter, Saavni. After Shanti reviews her information on the Review & Submit page she clicks, "Submit".

Splash Page

Shanti is given a message confirmation the successful submission of her leave request. She feels good knowing she simply needs to wait for her acknowledgement letter to arrive. Shanti is also pleased with the imagery once again. The visual gives her a very warm and comforting feeling. Shanti clicks "OK" and is taken back to the main landing page.

Landing Page

Upon arriving back at the main landing page, Shanti notices the addtion of an open leave card to the right of her other open leave. She can clearly tell from this that she needs to complete her paperwork as soon as it arrives in the mail. Shanti is curious what additional information she'd be able to see if she clicked on the "View Detais" button. She goes ahead and clicks it.

Details Page

Shanti is taken to a page with variout details related to her leave. There isn't anything she needs to currently take a closer look at here so she feels it's time to log out. Shanti finds the "Log Out" link in the corner and clicks it.

Logout Page

Upon logging out, Shanti once again sees the vibrant image she first saw upon logging in and is inspired to get outside for some fresh air.

User Testing

Overall, testing of these pages resulted in far more positive feedback than pre-redesign. At this point, there were some items that needed to be reworked again, such as the main landing page which was still causing some confusion specifically related to the tasks section. The mockups above of the landing and details pages are actually even more recent mockups that are several iterations in from when this huge project initially began. I do not have any of the reports for the usability testing available to show here.
