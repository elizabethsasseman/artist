The "TurboTax" of Settling Someone's Estate

Role

Entrepreneur & Designer

Purpose

To Explore My Unique Idea

Date

Spring 2018

After watching my family struggle to find important paperwork related to a loved one's estate, it became apparent that a simple process did not exist in the realm of estate-planning or in settling someone's estate. Initially I wanted to build a product designed to streamline the process of figuring out what to do when someone passes away. I considered this idea the "TurboTax" of settling someone's estate. The vision of my product evolved to include additional ideas, including a platform where value between clients and attorneys would be exchanged.

I initially thought it would be ideal to design a website that would walk clients step-by-step through the process of settling someone's estate after they've passed away. I thought the main goal should relate to the organization of documents online as opposed to on paper, and to allow clients to interact with attorneys virtually as opposed to in person.

The last thing family members want to do after a loved one passes away is go searching for lost documents while trying to determine what documents are actually needed and who needs them. This seemed like a problem to me, and it seemed like a problem worth taking a closer look at.

Brainstorm

I began asking friends and family for their thoughts on whether a step-by-step process seemed like something that would be helpful or not. It became clear that this sort of simplified process would be very helpful, but it was still very unclear the logistics of how a website product like this should actually function.

Through further brainstroming, I realized developing a legal document storage platform might be a good place to start at least at first. This would allow estate-planning attorneys and their clients to store and view documents virtually, thereby eliminating paper that could potentially get lost. But this type of product already exists so finding a way to incentivize a larger population of attorneys and their clients to store their documents virtually would be necessary.

I didn't want to build a product that would require me to hire attorneys or to be as knowledgeable as attorneys, I wanted to find a way to build a space somewhere between them. Below are some of the important questions I began to consider and discuss with people:

  • Who are my competitors? Storage software already exists, but how user friendly is it and what could make it better for clients and attorneys?
  • How could I talk to attorneys for research purposes without spending any of my own personal money?
  • Am I selling this to clients or law firms or both? Who will be paying us considering we have to make money somehow?
  • Other than attorneys, who else do clients go to when planning their estate?
  • What percent of clients are open to storing their estate plan in a cloud as opposed to at home?
  • Would it be possible to urge users to connect accounts like banking, social media and more in order to simplify the process of settling the person's estate once they've passed away?
  • Would it work to have users pay for this service after they've passed away?
  • Could I work out a deal with law firms where they give clients a discount in return for me promoting them within a directory on my platform?
  • What are my product's biggest selling points?
  • Will client's confidential information, such as bank accounts be stored on my platform or just linked?
  • How often do clients even use an attorney's services while planning their estate?
  • Are there any uncomfortable topics that might come up while settling a loved one's estate such as memberships or subscriptions to places the person might not want family to know about? Is there a way for my app to help eliminate this kind of conflict?
  • Is there any way I can offer my product to clients for free? And if so, indefinitely or just for awhile?
  • Is there a huge inconvenience that exists in estate-planning attorneys jobs that I could find a way for my platform to solve?
  • How should I market my platform? What kind of feel should it have, considering this is a very serious and sensitive subject?
  • Should my platform include a will-maker, preliminary questionnaire or other services for clients prior to meeting with their attorney?

Discuss

I decided to meet with my friend, Josh who is a software engineer to discuss the constraints related to developing a website platform like this. Josh's biggest concern was security. A secure website would equate to client confidence in my product. A lack of security would be a huge problem.

Before doing any research, I reassessed where I was at in my design and realized I could structure my platform to include two types of participants who would exchange value in some way. With my current idea, I didn't have a clear idea of where value would exist in my platform. Restructuring it to allow for a value exchange between participants really helped create a clear idea of how value could potentially flow between users.

I also realized this platform could be marketable for more than just estate-planning. Although I might still initially market to only this niche, I wanted to keep in mind the entire market while researching.

At this point, I wanted my research to help me find out what I could solve for law firms and what I could solve for clients. Ultimatey, if I could build one platform solving problems on both sides, I'd have a valuable and marketable product.

Research

I decided cold-calling law firms would be a helpful research method in answering some important initial questions. I also needed to talk to people who fit the persona of prospective clients in order to determine what would make their lives easier when working with an attorney. I began by talking with friends and co-workers.

Analyze

After talking with attorneys and receptionists at law firms in a bunch of different states, I became aware of a handful of inconveniences that exist in the daily job of attorneys. Although some inconveniences are very specific to certain disciplines or certain sizes of law firms, the general inconveniences shared between everyone is a lack of integration between software products used for daily tasks such as document storage, client intake, calendar, email and accounting. There isn't one affordable software product that integrates all of these daily tasks. Part of this has to do with how different the tasks are between firms and between sizes of firms. Salesforce, for example is closer to solving this integration problem but is very expensive and smaller firms can't afford it.

After talking with people about what would make their lives easier when working with attorneys I found out people desire an easier-to-understand process in regards to estate-planning specifically as well as a cheaper process. Younger people felt the ability to connect with attorneys within an app would be great.

Brainstorm

Although I knew I would need to do more research, my current research had already helped me solve the chicken-or-egg-first problem that exists in starting a platform business. This problem tends to be difficult to solve. The chicken-or-egg-first problem for my platform idea exists because clients won't sign up to use my platform if there aren't any attorneys already available on my platform to provide services, and attorneys won't sign up if the demand from clients to use their services don't already exist on my platform.

I solved this by finding a way to provide an initial service to both sides which wouldn't have either side dependent on the other. This initial service would be free document storage. And I hoped to find a way to provide it within an environment that also helps solve some of the integration problems attorneys face. I would start by offering this free document storage to both attorneys and their clients. Once the platform of users on both sides grows, we'd implement our platform feature which would allow clients to search for and connect with attorneys. This would hopefully help retain these clients as future customers, inspire them to tell their friends and provide us with an actual source of income. Hopefully. Theoretically, we would make money because in return for free document storage, attorneys would be required to have clients pay via our platform. And clients would be charged a separate fee which would go to our business.

With these simple functions in mind, I began to create some quick sketched wireframes displaying the more marketable features of my app. You can see these below.

Large Font Sizes

With a large market existing in estate-planning, the interfaces needed to have large, easy-to-read fonts. The amount of verbal information on each page would also be kept to a minimum in order to minimize the amount of reading necessary.

Easily Viewable Documents

The ability to view and manage documents within a highly user-friendly environment would be extremely important considering free document storage is one of the platforms biggest selling points.

Integration of Daily Tasks

The app would eliminate the need for law firms to use more than one software product for daily tasks such as client intake, calendar, email accounting and document storage. Attorneys would be able to navigate between tasks without needing to "copy and paste" information from one software product to another.

Next Steps

At this point, I needed to find a way to perform more intensive research, determine whether or not I'd be able to pull any APIs into my product, and decide whether or not I wanted to pursue starting a business or reach out to some incubators. If I wasn't going to be able to pull any APIs, I wouldn't want to build a product with these features from scratch. And if I couldn't solve the integration problem for law firms, I would need to determine whether document storage and an app to connect clients to attorneys would be enough. I also needed to consider whether the product would make enough money to cover the free document storage I'd be offering. I knew drafting up a business plan would be one of the next steps if I really wanted to get after this idea!
