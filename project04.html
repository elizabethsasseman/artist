Designed a Mobile UI Flow

This project displays a flow of mobile mockups leading a user to view and then submit an intermittent time off request related to a leave of absence from work. In order to show a users' flow through an action, series of three screens are displayed at a time.

Justin has logged into his absence management portal for his leave from work. He needs to check the status of a current request for intermittent time off as well as put in an additional intermittent time off request. In the first mockup below, Justin arrives on the Leave Details page. He scrolls down to find the Intermittent Time off Requests section (second mockup). He immediately recognizes that his current requests are the dates that have dots under them. He taps the 26th and the screen changes to show the request for this date (third mockup). He is now able to see the status of this request. Justin taps the back arrow to return to the main calendar view.

Now that Justin can see the main calendar view again (first mockup), he needs to add his request. He needs to add it for the 7th of February. Because the numbers on the calendar are consistently blue like links and buttons on the rest of the site, he knows they're all clickable. He taps the 7th and is taken instead this time to fill in details for his request. He goes ahead and fills out the form (second and third mockups). He then clicks the blue Submit button at the bottom.

After clicking the Submit button he gets a confirmation message verifying that his request has been submitted (first mockup). He clicks the blue back button to get back to the main calendar view and now sees a blue dot under the 7th (second mockup). Just to double-check to make sure everything looks right, he taps the 7th and can now view his request (third mockup).
